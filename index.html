Map your hearing,
tone by tone.

An adaptive pure-tone hearing screen that runs entirely in your browser. It steps frequencies up and down to find your threshold at each pitch, masks the non-test ear, sneaks in silent catch-trials to check you're answering honestly, and draws your audiogram — no app, no upload, no server.

Best with calibrated over-ear headphones in a quiet room. This is an experiment for self-exploration, not a medical diagnosis — see an audiologist for a clinical assessment.

what it does
More than a beep test.
📉

Adaptive staircase

Tones get quieter when you hear them and louder when you miss, converging on your threshold (in dB HL) per frequency.

🔀

Contralateral masking

A second phase plays soft noise in the non-test ear so a good ear can't "cross-hear" for the weaker one.

🤫

Silent catch-trials

Hidden windows of pure silence. Responses to them are run through a binomial test to flag random guessing.

📊

Your audiogram

Left/right thresholds for both pure-tone and masked phases are plotted on a clinical-style audiogram you can re-test.
